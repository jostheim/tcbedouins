Where are we meeting next?

Twin Cities Bedouins is a traveling group of people who create.

One of the problems most often mentioned by members of the Minneapolis/St. Paul tech scene about the Minneapolis/St. Paul tech scene is the lack of success getting quality startups going in town. It is our belief that the main issue with pushing forward is not a lack of Angel Funding, a lack of talent or a lack of will. We think the reason is simply a lack of momentum. In physics Momentum = Mass * Velocity, and we think that translates pretty directly to the tech scene in Minneapolis/St. Paul as well.

In Silicon Valley everyone works in high tech, and many work at startups. You can't go to a coffee shop (according to Luke Franci, a Y-Combinator alum who spent a summer in Silicon Valley) without running into folks coding, talking tech or working in some way on a startup. Contrast that with Minneapolis where you are only guaranteed to run into another entrepreneur if you scheduled a meeting or you hit Minnebar or Minnedemo (which occur about 4 times a year, and by the way are absolutely awesome events). In short there is no Momentum, we lack both Mass (people) and Velocity (frequency of events).

Twins Cities Bedouins is meant to help remedy this situation. Based on an idea born in Austin, Cafe Bedouins is a group of self-starters, entrepeneurs, coders, hackers, and side-project builders (to name a few) who get together at a coffee shop once a week to hack, code, colloborate, and network (to name a few things). Once a week to keep the Velocity side of the equation going and a group to feed the Mass side of the equation.

Our sincere hope is that Twin Cities Bedouins helps get ideas built, people networked, and overall greases the wheels of the Minneapolis/St. Paul tech startup community.

Nate and Jamie (organizers)

Where do we meet?

In general we will meet at coffee shops around town. Our goal is to have a good geographic distribution of sites to meet at so the meetings will be convenient to attend no matter where you live. We are currently working on finalizing a list of potential locations, please contribute any good locations you know about!

What to bring?

Bring whatever materials you need to hack! This is not a formal thing so just bring whatever feels right, for example:

  1. laptop
  2. moleskin
  3. pencil
  4. your brain
  5. a bit of money to support the coffee shop

What to expect?

TC Bedouins is informal, so there are not any speakers or formal agenda, most we might have is name tags. Mostly we expect there to be some people talking, some people staring at screens, some people hacking and some people just gabbing. In short there is no right or wrong!

Best way to talk about TC Bedouins with other Bedouins (including the organizers) is the Google Group.
